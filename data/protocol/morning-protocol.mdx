Wake, move, focus. A zero-decision morning system designed to run on autopilot — every day starts the same way, regardless of what happens after. The goal is to arrive at your desk having already trained, eaten, and planned your day before most people are awake.

**Wake time:** Same time every day, no exceptions — including weekends
**Structure:** Wake → Active commute → Train → Plan → Eat → Work
**Duration:** ~2 hours from alarm to desk

---

## The Sequence

| Offset | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| T+0 | Wake up. Get out of bed immediately — no snooze, no phone. |
| T+5 min | Greens supplement with water on an empty stomach. Get dressed. |
| T+15 min | Leave the house. Active commute to gym (walking, cycling, etc.) — this is your cardio warm-up. |
| T+40 min | Arrive at gym, change. |
| T+45 to T+105 | Workout (60 min). |
| T+105 to T+120 | Sauna + cold plunge (sauna days) or shower. |
| T+120 | Arrive at desk. |
| T+120 to T+150 | Daily planning — review task manager, set priorities for the day (30 min). |
| T+150 | Breakfast. Take fat-soluble supplements (D3+K2, Omega-3) with food. |

The first 90 minutes are physical — wake, move, train. No screens, no decisions, no thinking about work. By the time you sit down, your body is warm, your mind is clear, and you've already accomplished something meaningful.

### Why This Order Works

**Training first thing** means it never gets bumped by meetings, deadlines, or energy dips. You'll never "not feel like it" at 6 AM because you're not awake enough to debate yourself. Just execute.

**Active commute as warm-up** replaces the treadmill. 15-25 minutes of brisk walking or cycling is zone 1-2 cardio that primes your body for training. By the time you touch a barbell, you're already warm.

**Planning before breakfast** means you set your intentions while your mind is freshest. Don't check email or Slack first — that lets other people set your priorities. Open your task manager, review what matters, then eat.

## Before Leaving the House

Pack the night before (this is part of the evening protocol), but do a quick check on the way out:

- Greens supplement taken (empty stomach)
- Gym bag — should always be packed and ready:
  - Gym clothes + towel
  - Lifting shoes (on heavy squat/deadlift days)
  - Headphones
  - Water bottle
- Daily supplements (from weekly pill organizer — grab today's slot)
- Breakfast container (if eating at your desk)
- Work bag if needed (laptop, charger)
- Banana or small snack for pre-workout energy
- Phone, wallet, keys

**Refill the pill organizer every Sunday** during weekly prep — 7 days of stacks ready for the week. One less decision every morning.

## Daily Planning (30 min)

This happens at your desk before you open email, Slack, or any communication tool. The goal is to decide what *you* want from the day before the world tells you what *it* wants.

### The Process

1. **Review your task manager** (5 min) — What's due? What carried over from yesterday? What's the highest-impact thing you could do today?
2. **Set 1-3 priorities** (5 min) — These are the things that, if you accomplished nothing else today, would make it a good day. Write them down where you'll see them.
3. **Check your calendar** (5 min) — What meetings do you have? Where are the deep work blocks? Are there any conflicts to resolve?
4. **Time-block your day** (10 min) — Assign your priorities to specific blocks. Protect your deep work time. Move or decline meetings that conflict with your most important work.
5. **Start your first deep work block** (5 min transition) — Open only what you need. Close everything else. Begin.

### Rules

- **No email or messaging before planning is done.** Checking email first thing means you're reacting to other people's priorities instead of setting your own.
- **No more than 3 priorities per day.** If everything is a priority, nothing is. Pick the 1-3 things that actually move the needle.
- **If you don't plan the day, the day plans you.** Thirty minutes of intentional planning saves hours of reactive scrambling.

## Mid-Morning Snack

Around 2-3 hours after breakfast, have a protein bar or similar high-protein snack (~200 cal, 20g protein). This bridges the gap between breakfast and lunch without requiring any preparation or decision-making.

The goal is to keep protein intake distributed across the day (4-5 feedings at 20-50g each) for optimal muscle protein synthesis. See the Nutrition Protocol for the full framework.

## The Non-Negotiable

**Same wake time every day** — weekdays, weekends, holidays. Your circadian rhythm doesn't know what day it is. The single most impactful thing you can do for sleep quality, morning energy, and long-term consistency is to anchor your wake time.

If you went to bed late, still wake up at the same time. You'll be tired — and that's the point. You'll naturally go to bed earlier that night, self-correcting without shifting your entire schedule. The moment you start sleeping in on weekends, you're giving yourself jet lag every Monday.

---

## What This Replaces

Before this protocol, mornings were a negotiation: *Should I work out? When? Where? What should I eat? Should I check email first?* Every decision burned willpower before the day even started.

Now there are zero decisions. The sequence is fixed, the bag is packed, the food is prepped. You wake up and execute. The thinking happened once — when you designed the protocol. Every morning after that is just running the program.
