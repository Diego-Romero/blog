Wind down, reflect, prepare for tomorrow. The evening protocol has two versions — a full 60-minute ritual for quiet nights, and a compressed 30-minute version for nights when you get home late from social activities. The core idea: close the day intentionally, empty your brain onto paper, and set tomorrow up so the morning protocol runs on autopilot.

**Start time:** 2.5-3 hours before your target wake time (adjust based on your sleep target)
**Two modes:** Standard (60 min) and Compressed (30 min)
**Goal:** Arrive in bed with a quiet mind, a packed bag, and zero open loops

---

## Standard Wind-Down (60 min)

For quiet evenings — no social plans, home by dinner time.

### 1. Close the Day & Prep Tomorrow (10 min)

- Quick tidy — put things back in place, clear surfaces
- Pack gym bag (clothes, towel, water bottle, headphones)
- Lay out tomorrow's outfit
- Check calendar for tomorrow's first commitment
- Pack work bag if needed (laptop, charger)

This isn't deep cleaning — it's a 10-minute reset. The apartment doesn't need to be perfect. You're closing loops: everything goes back where it belongs, tomorrow's logistics are handled, and you won't waste any morning willpower on decisions.

### 2. Journal (20 min)

Open a notebook. No phone, no screens.

Write whatever comes to mind — no rules, no prompts, no structure required. Just dump what's in your head onto paper. If nothing comes, start with what happened today and see where it goes.

**Why this works:** The things that keep you awake at night are usually unfinished thoughts looping in your head. Once they're on paper, they stop looping. You've externalized them — they'll be there tomorrow if they matter. Your brain can let go.

Some useful prompts if you need a starting point:

- **What happened today?** — Just the facts. What did you do, who did you talk to, what stood out.
- **What went well?** — Wins, even small ones. Training PR, good meeting, made someone laugh.
- **What's unresolved?** — What's bothering you, what's incomplete, what's looping.
- **What did you notice about yourself?** — One pattern, reaction, or insight from today.

Don't optimize the journal. Don't make it a productivity tool. It's a brain dump. The messier and more honest, the more effective.

### 3. Read (20 min)

- Take bedtime supplements (magnesium, zinc, ashwagandha — see Nutrition Protocol)
- Phone on charger — outside the bedroom, or face-down on Do Not Disturb
- Dim all lights to warm/amber only
- Activate mattress cooling (if you have temperature-controlled bedding)
- Read a physical book — no screens, no backlit e-readers
- Lights out when drowsy

The reading isn't for productivity or learning — it's a transition ritual. Your brain associates the sequence (dim lights → supplements → book) with "time to sleep." After a few weeks, you'll start feeling drowsy within 10 minutes of picking up the book. That's the point.

## Compressed Wind-Down (30 min)

For nights when you get home late from social activities, classes, or events.

### 1. Cool Down (10 min)

- Quick shower (lukewarm → cool rinse to drop core temperature)
- Hydrate — water or electrolytes
- Lay out tomorrow's outfit + confirm gym bag is packed

### 2. Journal (10 min)

Quick brain dump — whatever's on your mind. Shorter than the standard version, but don't skip it entirely. Even 5 minutes of writing helps clear the mental noise from a stimulating evening.

### 3. Lights Out (10 min)

- Bedtime supplements
- Phone away, lights dim, mattress cooling on
- Read a few pages if you want, or go straight to sleep

The compressed version exists because **some wind-down is infinitely better than no wind-down**. On busy social nights, it's tempting to skip everything and crash into bed wired. The 30-minute version gives your nervous system just enough deceleration to transition from high-energy socializing to sleep.

## Evening Checklist

Use this as a quick mental scan before bed — whether standard or compressed:

- Kitchen clean, things put away
- Gym bag packed for tomorrow (clothes, towel, water bottle, headphones)
- Tomorrow's outfit laid out
- Calendar checked — know when your first commitment is
- Work bag packed if needed
- Journal done (brain dump)
- Bedtime supplements taken
- Phone on charger — outside bedroom or DND face-down
- Mattress cooling activated (if applicable)
- Lights dimmed — warm/amber only

---

## Why Two Versions

A rigid 60-minute wind-down that you skip 3 nights a week is worse than a flexible system with a compressed fallback. Life has social events, late dinners, classes, and spontaneous plans. The protocol needs to accommodate that.

**The rule:** On nights with evening activities, use the compressed version. On quiet nights, use the standard version. But **always do one of the two**. Never go from high stimulation straight to bed with nothing in between.

Over a typical week, you might run the standard version 4 nights and the compressed version 3 nights. Both end the same way: brain dumped, bag packed, lights dim, book in hand.

## What This Replaces

Before this protocol, evenings were unstructured. Some nights I'd journal, some nights I'd scroll my phone in bed. Gym bag got packed in the morning (stealing 10 minutes of decision-making from the protocol that needs it least). Supplements were inconsistent — sometimes remembered, sometimes not.

Now the evening is a system that feeds into the morning. Tomorrow's logistics are handled tonight. The journal clears your head so you fall asleep faster. The supplements are part of a sequence, not a thing to remember. And the morning protocol starts seamlessly because everything was set up the night before.
